Corona Virus (COVID-19)

This article is incomplete

Overview

Corona Virus is a series of diseases reported by the CDC (Center for Disease Control) COVID-19 is the 2019 variation of the disease that was first spotted in December of 2019. It is part of the Flu family. CoronaVirus has the symptoms of the common cold, or common Flu, and has 2 common variants (Cold, and Flu)

The Corona Virus first started spreading in Wuhan China in December of 2019. The Chinese government limits what information comes in and out of China, and their numbers are likely not completely accurate. The disease was rumored to start after consumption of bat meat from a store.

In 2020, the Corona Virus spread around the world, and became a Pandemic. Fears of the virus were growing fast, and the fear was spreading faster than the Virus itself. The CDC classified the Corona Virus as a pandemic, and as of March 9th 2020, it affects over 100 countries.

The Corona virus has proven deadly to people over the age of 30, especially the elderly. People under the age of 30 report to recover from the virus easier. The Corona Virus has a 2% mortality rate. Kids can still get the Corona Virus, but there are no reported deaths so far for people under the age of 30 for COVID-19, except for infants and toddlers.

Several countries were not prepared for the Corona virus, and testing is still an issue in March of 2020. There were travel bans to and from countries including China, Iran, and Italy (US) and some people were put into quarantine for up to 28 days (14 on average)

Some passengers on cruise ships contracted the virus, and the whole ship got quarantined at bay, letting the disease spread faster. People have been taking precautions for the virus by washing their hands more, trying not to touch their face as much, and wearing face masks.

In March of 2020, all around the world, businesses and schools were being closed. Millions of people became unemployed, as they couldn't work from home, and global markets crashed over and over. Before the April update of this document, no children have died of COVID-19. This has changed, as now people under the age of 30 have reported to get it, and some have died from it.

Countries across the world are in lockdown, and Internet traffic has skyrocketed. While all this was going on, the world seemed in peril, and other things were being ignored, such as the EARN IT act, that was being snuck into COVID-19 bills in the United States.

As of April 10th 2020, there have been over 1.69 million (1,696,139) cases worldwide, with over 102,000 (102,669) deaths.

Chinese response

The Chinese government denied the virus for a while before it began to spread into a pandemic. Some odd things occurred at the start of the pandemic, including the mass construction of Chinese hospitals.

In China, many people were quarantined, and dragged out of areas by government services, some being treated like animals. This raised concerns over Chinas handling of the pandemic.

United States Response

The United States started to experience the Corona virus pandemic in February 2020 and cases began to rise. Response was very poor, and even as of March, the US was extremely unprepared. Test kits were in short supply, and misinformation spread like wildfire, causing country-wide panic, and turning affected cities into ghost towns.

The executive branch classified the virus as a Hoax, and was criticized with many other things related to the executive response. The response was very poor, and funding to the CDC was cut, leading to further controvery, and also making the United States even less prepared to help and end the pandemic.

Due to the poor response, and the false "everything is fine" response, fear spread wildly. The New York stock market crashed multiple times due to fear of buying Chinese products and getting the Corona virus. Panic buying spread across America, and stores were cleared out of key items quickly, most commonly canned food, toilet paper, and cleaning tools.

The country has been in lockdown, as of April 9th 2020, we are still in lockdown. The president constantly took sides on this, denying it, then realizing it exists, repeatedly over and over. He also tried to open the country up by Easter, but this was denied.

Social distancing

A practice to prevent the spread of COVID-19 during the pandemic was social distancing, where people were not supposed to gather in groups, and stay at least 6 feet apart from each other.

A lot of people rebelled against this. People attention-hungry went up and coughed on people, and many church leaders went against the government and held large gatherings in their services, despite warnings, and got major fines.,/p>

Quarantine

Quarantine was a big part of the pandemic. Countries that recognized this urged people to stay at home. Some countries took a lot longer to pick up on this, like the United States. People would still ignore this in some ways and continue to go out.

Overtime, schools and businesses closed, and unemployment skyrocketed around the globe. Most restaurants became drive-thru and pick-up only. People stayed at home after stocking up supplies. Of the supplies that were bought up, toilet paper, cleaning agents, and canned foods were purchased the most often.

Quarantine life was difficult for people who aren't used to staying at home. Many workers couldn't work from home, and continued to work, unless they were fired. Meanwhile, jobs like programming, banking, etc were also difficult at home, due to the work environment being different. Nonetheless, these people still had jobs though, unlike others.

Online schools took off, one particular software for conferencing that gained widespread use was Zoom, which was later revealed to have tons of privacy and security issues. Many states and businesses began banning the software. Those who kept it often had things like Pornographic raids happen, where someone got the password to the room and began to stream sexual actions in front of the whole virtual conference room.

Quarantine life was hard on a lot of people. Celebrities began to profit off of this, and complain about being home all day. Unlike before the quarantine, people hated on celebrities that did this, rather than supporting them.
